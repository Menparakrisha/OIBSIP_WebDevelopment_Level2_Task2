William Shakespeare

1564 – 1616

“All the world's a stage, and all the men and women merely players.”

Biography

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet, and actor widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world's greatest dramatist. Often called the “Bard of Avon,” his works include 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and several poems.

His masterpieces such as Hamlet, Macbeth, Romeo and Juliet, and Othello continue to inspire millions around the world. Shakespeare transformed literature with his deep understanding of human emotion, creativity, and storytelling.
